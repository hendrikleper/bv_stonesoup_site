Kunstenaars

#10   Dirk Zoete (BE)

Het werk van Dirk Zoete bestaat vooral uit tekeningen, schaalmodellen en installaties. Veelal gaat het in de maquettes over de omgang met architectuur en de basiselementen van leven en wonen. Energie (warmte, licht, water), ruimte en landschap worden op minder voor de hand liggende manieren onderzocht en soms op absurde manieren aangewend.
Sinds een tijd experimenteert Dirk met scenografische elementen die fungeren als architecturale objecten waarbinnen een resem obscure ‘figuranten’ zich manifesteren. Deze speelse transformaties alluderen op een fluïde identiteit en de dislocatie van de plek als gevolg van dit gebeuren. Vormelijk is er zichtbare invloed van kunstenaars zoals onder andere Malevich en Moholy Nagy; en de kleurrijke, abstracte gedaantes glijden in de ruimte tussen teken-, schilder- en beeldhouwkunst. Door dergelijke elementen te activeren en op straat te halen treedt een vervreemding op die anders ervaren wordt in vergelijking met een soortgelijk gebeuren binnen de muren van een museum of kunstgalerie.

#09   Will Brown (USA)

Will Brown is a collaborative project formerly based in a storefront space in San Francisco’s Mission District. Our main objective is to manipulate the structures of exhibition-making as a critical practice. Will Brown is Lindsey White, Jordan Stein, and David Kasprzak.
In addition to their HQ storefront program, Will Brown has realized projects with Kadist Foundation, Wattis Institute, di Rosa, Southern Exposure, and has a forthcoming exhibition at the Berkeley Art Museum in the summer of 2015. WB received an Alternative Exposure Award from Southern Exposure in 2012 and in 2013 was an artist-in-residence at Headlands Center for the Arts in Marin, California.

#08   Diane Rabreau (FR)

Diane Rabreau (1990) studeerde animatiefilm aan ENSAD in Parijs en mediakunst aan het KASK in Gent. Ze reist sindsdien doorheen Europa, gedreven door op het eerste zicht banale onregelmatigheden in Google Maps die haar via haar website worden gemeld. Om een vraag te beantwoorden zoekt Diane de desbetreffende plek op en onderzoekt ze die grondig. Dat leidt tot verrassende, poëtische en vaak hilarische blogposts, videos, dagboekfragmenten, interviews en tekeningen.

#07   Clare Noonan (NZL)

Clare Noonan was born in 1982 in Christchurch, New Zealand. Clare works with a variety of media including photography, sculpture and video. Clare is a member of the collaboration All the Cunning Stunts and is represented Motive Gallery, Brussels. She currently lives and works in Brussels, Belgium.

#05   Honoré d’O (BE)

After studying architecture Honoré δ’O disappears from the social scene and develops in seven years of silence a personal visual language. Honoré does not move, he works in silence. He looks, he thinks, he looks at a face, the white sheet, the silver screen, the frame and the base, and wonders which medium will carry what kind of information from where to where, what history will reveal itself, what the word destination means, how culture pretends to be civilized. He marvels mainly about the miracle of man and creation, and the limitations of language to visualize ‘commitment’.
He hitchhikes across Europe to visits the major museums of fine arts. He finds himself suddenly in Split or Dubrovnik, Granada or Dusseldorf. He walks through Greece to the tip of the Peloponnese, and afterwards locks himself up in small workshops and student rooms, which quite naturally transform into philosophical laboratories and spatial experiments. There he developed the belief that the classical distance between the artwork and the viewer can be lifted. Not the thing, but the relationship between things and the user, between the environment and its participant can be added to the existence by means of the visual.

#03   Tinka Pittoors (BE)

The landscape, a patchwork of elements dominating each other, a precarious balance stretching out around us, composed of contradictions, both human and natural. A constant striving for power, utopian domination.
My sculptures and objects arise from this perception and examine the utopia of a malleable world, a reflection on landscape as a metaphor for a condition humaine. The landscape is presented as a plane for the projection of desires and clichés. Its surface is frayed, cut up and glued back together again in a renewed scenery that blurs boundaries between inside and out. This condition implies a constant pushing along, a contortion of form and movement.
By combining recognizable, everyday forms and materials with my own constructions, a new and unique language of form is created. Sculptures and installations function like neologisms: they are grafted onto situations we know, but exist in and create a parallel sculptural reality. Whereas the materials seek to connect with reality, the form actually rebels against it.
Lighting plays a significant role in this process: it encompasses and defines the sculpture, but at the same time underlines the autonomy of the world presented. An ambiguous image is created that balances on the verge of reality. Whether the resulting proposals are utopian or dystopian is left aside.
For us, the question remains: is the temptation of the image really inhabitable?

#01   Caroline Vincart (BE)

As an artist Caroline is developing a discourse on the statute of the photographic wherein several means and limitations articulate its showing process. She uses parameters like ‘distance’ and ‘intimacy’ to create a framework in which her work can exist and come into existence. She explores the boundaries of abstraction and anecdotical situations, yet is most of all fascinated by what lies in between.
